# Fundamentals of Innovation Literacy - Framework Summary

## The 11 Elements

### The What (1-3)
1. **Process** - Structured, repeatable activity (Design Thinking framework)
2. **Interventions** - Stage-dependent methods that advance the process
3. **Tools** - Physical objects and technologies (pencils, Miro/Mural, AI assistants)

### The How (4-9)
4. **Skills and Competencies** - Analytical thinking, creative thinking, communication, systems thinking, experimentation, comfort with ambiguity
5. **Mindset** - Curiosity, tolerance for uncertainty, challenging assumptions, holding multiple perspectives
6. **Behaviours** - Active listening, constructive challenge, disciplined experimentation, reflective practice
7. **Environment** - Physical spaces, temporal boundaries, cultural conditions that protect innovation behaviours from operational gravity
8. **Collaboration** - Psychological safety, cognitive diversity, constructive conflict, distributed cognition, role clarity
9. **Facilitation** - Unlocking potential of processes and collaboration, managing conflict, enabling learning

### The Why (10-11)
10. **Governance** - Bidirectional communication infrastructure mediating shared organisational purpose; formal mechanisms (charters, decision rights) that insulate innovation from operational expectations
11. **Resources** - Time, budget, people, materials; contested against operations, validated through governance, justified using Elements 1-9 as evidence

---

## Key Distinctions

**Tools vs Interventions:**
- Tools = what you use (Miro, pencils, AI)
- Interventions = how you're instructed to use them ("dig deeper", "don't defend your prototype")

**Environment vs Governance:**
- Environment = physical/temporal/cultural conditions (the container)
- Governance = bidirectional communication infrastructure and formal mechanisms (the legitimising structure)

**Governance vs Resources:**
- Governance = establishes shared why and validates innovation's legitimacy (the agreement)
- Resources = material commitments flowing from that validation (the tangible proof)

---

## Positioning

Innovation fails 70% of the time because organisations build management structures without process capability, or teach process skills without organisational defence capability.

This workshop addresses both:
- **Elements 1-9:** Process literacy (execution capability)
- **Elements 10-11:** Organisational capability (securing and defending what innovation requires)

## Workshop Format

**Duration:** 4 sessions across 2 days (one week apart)
- Day 1: 9:00-11:00, 14:00-16:00
- Day 2: 9:30-11:00, 14:00-16:00
- Total: 7h 40m

**Method:** Experiential learning through multiple micro-cycles (IDEO Wallet Project, Gift-Giving Project). Framework handoffs delivered after participants experience the need. Theory supports practice, doesn't replace it.

**Structure:**
- Day 1: Elements 1-9 experienced through Wallet Project + reflection/analysis
- Day 2: Elements 1-9 reinforced through Gift-Giving Project + Elements 10-11 through charter creation and value demonstration
