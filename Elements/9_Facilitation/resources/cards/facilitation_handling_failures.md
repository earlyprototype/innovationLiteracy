# Facilitation: Handling AI Failures

## When AI Produces Nonsense

**Don't apologise or skip — frame as diagnostic moment.**

1. **Pause and acknowledge:** "This output is nonsense. That's useful."
2. **Facilitate analysis:** "What did we ask for? What did we get? What's the mismatch?"
3. **Diagnose prompt logic:** "What constraint did we fail to specify?"
4. **Iterate visibly:** Refine prompt as group, compare outputs
5. **Extract learning:** "What does this teach us about articulating intent?"

## When AI Validates Everything

**Call it out — suspiciously neat outputs are red flags.**

1. **Name the pattern:** "This confirms all our assumptions. That's a red flag."
2. **Seek tension:** "Let's prompt AI to generate counter-arguments"
3. **Source check:** "Which user feedback supports this?"
4. **Reframe:** "We need critique, not confirmation"

## When Participants Want AI to Decide

**Redirect ownership.**

1. **Acknowledge:** "I hear you wanting AI to tell us which is best. That's tempting."
2. **Clarify limitation:** "AI can't tell you what matters for YOUR users in YOUR context"
3. **Redirect:** "AI synthesised options — which serves your users best and why?"
4. **Make visible:** Document decision rationale, not just AI output

## Troubleshooting Quick Reference

| Symptom | Diagnosis | Intervention |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Jumping to AI immediately | Misunderstanding AI role | "Human work first" sequencing |
| Long silences reading output | Underestimated interpretation time | Budget adequate time, normalise |
| Generic, disconnected outputs | Insufficient prompt constraints | Prompt co-creation with constraints |
| AI confirming everything | Echo chamber (Mode 1) | Counter-argument generation, bias check |
| Asking AI to decide | Confusion about AI capability | Redirect ownership, clarify |
| Frustration with poor output | Failure framed as blocker | Reframe as diagnostic moment |
