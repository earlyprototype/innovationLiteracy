# ⚡ QUICK REF: Session AI Framing

## Opening Frame (Say This at Session Start)

> "AI is rendering engine for YOUR logic, not a solution generator. The quality of what comes out depends entirely on the quality of what you put in."

> "Bad AI output is useful — it shows us what we failed to specify clearly."

> "We're teaching two things simultaneously: innovation process AND AI-augmented practice. Both matter, both take time."

## Control Model

**Participant-controlled preferred** — builds capability.
Facilitator-controlled only when: demos, severe time pressure, tool complexity.

## Time Expectations

**AI does NOT save time in real-time sessions.**
- Generation is fast, interpretation is slow
- Budget MORE time than manual methods
- Speed gains: documentation, post-session synthesis, generating variations

## Permission Structure for Failures

Establish early:
- "It's OK if AI generates rubbish — that's diagnostic data"
- "Prompt iteration IS the work"
- "We learn from failures, not just successes"

## Hard Rules

1. **Human work first** — no AI until human thinking documented
2. **Test with humans, not AI** — no synthetic user substitution
3. **You own interpretation** — AI patterns, human significance
4. **Verify everything** — Four Gates every time
