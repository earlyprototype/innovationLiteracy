# Remote Facilitation Adaptations

## Core Principle

The principles of facilitation (equalising participation, managing energy, protecting process) remain identical remotely. The mechanisms change entirely. Remote isn't inferior — it's different.

## Key Adaptations

**Energy Management is Harder:** Static screen-staring drains faster. What works 90min in-person exhausts after 45min remotely.
- Sessions: 45-60min max
- Breaks: 10min every 45min
- Camera-optional during individual work
- Movement prompts during breaks

**Silence is Amplified:** On video, silence during thinking feels like failure. Narrate: "We're in 2 minutes of silent thinking — quiet is expected."

**Reading the Room:** No body language. Adapt with: energy checks ("thumbs up/sideways/down"), monitoring chat, polls for real-time feedback.

**Equalising is Easier — If Designed:** Remote naturally reduces HiPPO (everyone same size on screen). But people hide more easily. Use: explicit turn-taking, breakout rooms, chat for parallel contribution.

**Tools ARE the Workspace:** Digital boards (Mural/Miro) are the primary workspace, not peripheral. Technical fluency = prerequisite. Templates must be pre-built; improvisation is harder.

**Async is a Feature:** Enable between-session contribution: seed boards before sessions, leave work visible, invite commentary between meetings.

## Practical Tips

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Technical anxiety | 10min pre-session warmup |
| Energy drain | Cameras-off for deep work, movement breaks |
| Unequal participation | Structured turn-taking, breakout rooms |
| Large groups (5+) | Breakout → plenary → breakout cycles |
| Lost ideas in chat | Actively monitor + surface chat contributions |
| Disorientation | Pre-built templates, not blank canvases |
| Time blindness | On-screen timers, verbal time checks |
| Hybrid challenges | Everyone on individual screens, even if co-located |
