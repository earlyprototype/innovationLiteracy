# Active Listening & Constructive Challenge

## Why It Matters

Innovation requires the clash of cognitively diverse perspectives, which naturally produces friction. Active listening ensures ideas are actually heard. Constructive challenge ensures they're rigorously stress-tested without attacking the person.

## Key Principles

**Listening to Understand vs. Respond:** In operational meetings, people listen waiting for their turn. In innovation, active listening requires suspending your own agenda to fully integrate the other person's perspective.

**The "Yes, And..." Rule:** During divergent phases, ban "Yes, but..." (convergent evaluation in disguise). Force additive language: "Yes, and we could also..."

**Challenge the Idea, Not the Person:** During convergent phases, shift from personal to systemic critique. Instead of "That idea won't work," ask: "What must be true in the market/system for this idea to succeed?"

## Practical Application

- **Set explicit rules:** Establish as "Rules of Engagement" at workshop start
- **Assign a behavioural moderator:** Someone whose sole job is to call out "Yes, but..." during ideation
- **Correct gently and immediately:** Non-punitive correction builds behavioural muscle memory
