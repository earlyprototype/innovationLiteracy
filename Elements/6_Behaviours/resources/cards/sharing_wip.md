# Sharing Work in Progress

## Why It Matters

Ideas kept private until polished miss iterative improvement when course correction is still cheap. Yet most cultures punish unfinished work — "come back when it's ready" suppresses exactly the behaviour innovation requires.

## Key Principles

**The Vulnerability Paradox:** Sharing incomplete work feels risky (exposing half-formed thinking) but is actually risk-reducing (catching flawed assumptions early). The courage is front-loaded; value accrues throughout.

**Permission Must Be Explicit:** "In this space, rough beats polished" or "10% thinking is welcome." Without clear permission, operational defaults dominate.

**Visual Availability Transforms Collaboration:** Work visible on walls or shared boards can be passively absorbed and serendipitously connected. Ideas trapped in private files cannot be built upon.

**Quality Improves Through Iteration, Not Isolation:** Rough ideas + feedback > polished ideas developed alone.

**Feedback Must Match Fidelity:** Early-stage sharing requires generative feedback ("I noticed...", "Have you considered...") not evaluative feedback ("but what about...").

## Practical Application

- **Set ground rules:** "We share at 10% complete, not 90%"
- **Model from leadership:** Leaders who share half-formed ideas give permission
- **Create persistent visible workspaces:** Wall space, digital boards for ongoing visibility
- **Schedule "WIP Reviews":** "Here's where I'm stuck" sessions — for input, not evaluation
- **Celebrate the share, not the polish:** Reward transparency over presentation quality
