# What are Tools?

## Definition

Tools are the physical objects and useful technologies that enable innovation work. This includes everything from pencils and sticky notes to digital collaboration platforms and AI assistants. Tools are, for the sake of this workshop series, distinct from Interventions: tools are **what** you use; interventions are **how** you're instructed to use them. As such, document-based so called "toolkits" and method guides are signposted as resources as we touch on each of the various elements of innovation literacy.

## Why It Matters

Innovation requires practical enablers. The right tools make previously impossible work feasible - digital whiteboards enable remote collaboration, AI assistants expand observation and ideation capacity, physical prototyping materials make ideas tangible. 

Without appropriate tools, even well-designed processes stall. With the wrong tools, teams waste energy fighting infrastructure when they should be solving problems. 

The most common failure: mismatched tool fidelity. High-fidelity digital tools used too early force premature convergence. Low-fidelity tools used too late fail to provide the rigour needed for evaluation. Tool selection is process protection - matching the tool's constraints to the cognitive demands of the current phase.

## How It Shows Up in Practice

**Digital Collaboration**
- Miro, Mural (visual collaboration, asynchronous ideation)
- Zoom, Teams (synchronous communication)
- Slack, Discord (ongoing team coordination)

**Project & Task Management**
- Trello, Asana (Kanban workflows)
- Jira (development tracking)
- Notion, Obsidian, Airtable (flexible databases)

**AI Assistants**
- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini (research, ideation, synthesis)
- NotebookLM (research, document analysis and preparation)
- AI-enabled IDEs (development, prototyping (and all of the above))

**Physical Materials**
- Paper, pencils, markers
- Sticky notes, whiteboards
- Prototyping materials (tape, cardboard, pipe cleaners, scissors)

**Specialist Platforms**
- Survey tools (Google Forms, Typeform)
- Prototyping software (Figma, Sketch)
- Analytics platforms (Google Analytics, Hotjar)
