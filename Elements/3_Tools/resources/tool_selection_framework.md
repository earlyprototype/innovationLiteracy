# Tool Selection Framework

## Why It Matters
Innovation tools range from sticky notes to high-fidelity AI assistants. The most common failure mode in innovation practice is "tool fatigue" or deploying the wrong tool fidelity for the process stage. High-fidelity digital tools used too early force premature convergence, while low-fidelity tools used too late fail to provide the rigour needed for evaluation. Selecting the right tool is an act of process protection.

## Key Takeaways
- **Fidelity Matching:** Match the fidelity of the tool to the certainty of the idea. In the Empathise and Ideate phases, use low-fidelity tools (paper, whiteboards, pipe cleaners) because they lower the barrier to entry and nobody is afraid to throw them away. 
- **Tools Require Interventions:** A tool on its own does nothing. A digital whiteboard (Miro) is useless without a structured intervention (e.g., "50 ideas in 15 minutes") driving the interaction.
- **AI as a Synthesis Engine:** In the Define and Test phases, use AI not to *generate* the answers, but to synthesise large volumes of qualitative human data (interviews, feedback) to spot patterns you might miss.

## Practical Application Tips
- **Audit your current project toolkit:** Ensure the tools match the stage you are in.
- **Downgrade when diverging:** If you are in the Discovery phase and the team is building high-fidelity CAD models or polished Figma screens, stop them. Force the use of paper prototypes or rough sketches to ensure the team remains in an exploratory mindset.
- **Delay high-fidelity work:** Only permit the use of polished, high-fidelity tools once the fundamental problem and concept have been thoroughly validated.
