# Discovery (Empathise + Discover)

## The Expansion

The original Stanford d.school model called this stage "Empathise" — go out and understand users. The Design Council's Double Diamond calls it "Discover." In practice, the stage has expanded beyond pure empathy into broader investigation:

| Activity | Classic "Empathise" | Modern "Discovery" |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| User observation | ✓ | ✓ |
| Contextual interviews | ✓ | ✓ |
| Desk research & data analysis | — | ✓ |
| Trend scanning | — | ✓ |
| Analogous domain inspiration | — | ✓ |
| Stakeholder landscape mapping | — | ✓ |

The core principle remains: **understand before you solve.** The expansion acknowledges that understanding comes from multiple sources, not just direct user contact.

## What Happens Here

You are deliberately opening the first diamond. The job is to gather raw material — observations, data, surprises, contradictions — without filtering or evaluating it yet.

**The discipline:** Resist framing. Every observation that makes you think "so the problem is..." is premature. You're collecting, not concluding.

## Key Methods

- **Observation over interview:** Watch what people *do*, not what they *say* they do. The gap between the two is where insights live
- **"Why?" chains:** Five whys minimum. Surface answers hide structural causes
- **Empathy mapping:** What do users Say, Think, Do, Feel? The gaps between quadrants reveal unspoken needs
- **Extreme users first:** People at the edges of a behaviour reveal what mainstream users feel but can't articulate

## When to Stop

Discovery doesn't end when you run out of time — it ends when new observations stop surprising you. If the last 3 interviews produced no new themes, you've reached saturation. If every interview still reveals something unexpected, you're not done.

## Common Mistakes

- Conducting discovery to *confirm* a hypothesis rather than to *discover* one
- Treating a survey as empathy work (surveys measure; discovery explores)
- Stopping after one round because the timeline demands it
