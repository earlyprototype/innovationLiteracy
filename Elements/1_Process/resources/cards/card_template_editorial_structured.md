# Assumption Mapping Card Slide — Editorial Publishing Style (Structured)

## Design Philosophy
This slide should feel like a premium editorial layout from a design-forward publication. Think Kinfolk meets Harvard Business Review—sophisticated, considered, with each element earning its place through purpose rather than decoration.

The aesthetic is quiet confidence. Nothing shouts. Everything breathes. British English throughout, with a tone that respects the reader's intelligence whilst building their capability.

## Colour Palette
Base is warm cream (#FFF8F0) with depth and comfort. Premium uncoated stock quality.

Primary text is deep charcoal (#2C2C2C), soft for comfortable extended reading.

Primary accent is terracotta (#C1666B), used for editorial punctuation. Apply to underlines beneath key headings, primary emphasis, key quadrant highlights.

Secondary accent is bright warm amber (#F0B35F) for supporting elements, circle fills, timeline connectors, secondary highlights. Promotes warmth throughout.

## Typography System
This is where editorial craft shows.

Headings use a refined serif—something with character and warmth. The card title ("Assumption Mapping: Making the Implicit Testable") sits at 42-48pt with generous letterspacing. Section headers are 22-26pt, weighted medium.

Body text is a humanist sans-serif at 15-17pt, sized for comfortable reading with line height at 1.6. Never cramped, never floating.

Supporting text and captions use a lighter weight at 12-13pt, creating clear hierarchy through weight and size together.

Numbers use tabular lining figures in the body font, ensuring vertical alignment in the timeline.

## Compositional Structure
The layout uses asymmetry and intentional imbalance to create visual interest whilst maintaining readability.

**Header zone (top 18% of slide):**  
Title sits left-aligned with a thin terracotta underline (2px, extending 60% of title width). Metadata floats top-right in small caps, 11pt, amber. Generous space above and below creates breathing room.

**Content zone (middle 64%, asymmetric two-column):**  
Columns are 40:60 split for visual tension.

Left column (narrower, 40%): "Why It Matters" as a pulled quote style—larger body text (18pt) with increased leading. Below, "The Four Categories" as a four-part Venn diagram rendered in soft overlapping circles. Terracotta outlines at 2pt, fills at 8% opacity. Centre intersection highlighted with amber at 15% opacity. Labels positioned outside circles in small caps.

Right column (wider, 60%): "The Riskiest Assumption Framework" presented as an elegant 2×2 matrix. No heavy borders—use subtle amber rules (1pt) to separate quadrants. Top-right cell gets a terracotta tint (10% opacity) with "Test Immediately" in medium weight. Formula "Risk = Uncertainty × Impact" set in italic above matrix. Axis labels in small caps, rotated 90° for vertical axis.

**Process zone (bottom 18%):**  
Five-step timeline runs as a elegant horizontal sequence. Numbers in circles (terracotta stroke, no fill) connected by a single hairline rule in amber. Step labels positioned below in sentence case. Timeline sits 2/3 across slide width, leaving right third empty—intentional asymmetry.

Key Principles anchor bottom-left as a tight list with terracotta square bullets (4pt), creating visual balance with the timeline.

## Editorial Craft Details
Subtle details that signal quality without demanding attention.

Use a 8-column grid with 16px gutters, but break it intentionally. Perfect alignment is corporate; considered asymmetry is editorial.

Generous margins: 72px left and right, 64px top and bottom. Content has room to breathe.

Visual hierarchy through scale relationships: 3:2 ratio between title and section heads, 7:5 between section heads and body.

The Venn diagram and matrix should feel hand-considered, not template-generated. Slight optical adjustments to circle sizing. Numbers positioned for visual balance, not mechanical centering.

## What Makes This Editorial
Restraint in colour application—terracotta and amber appear as warm accents, never dominating. The warm cream base does most of the visual work.

Whitespace as active element, not leftover space. Each gap is intentional.

Typography-led hierarchy. Use size, weight, and spacing to create structure.

Organic forms (circles) balanced with geometric structure (grid, matrix), creating tension between systems.

## Content Treatment
Extract all content from the Assumption Mapping card source. The four categories appear in the Venn with clear labeling. The risk matrix shows accurate axes with proper quadrant identification. The 5-step process maintains sequence integrity. Key Principles remain visible but don't compete for attention.

This is teaching through considered design—each visual choice supports comprehension by reducing cognitive load whilst maintaining engagement through editorial craft.
