# Test

## What Happens Here

You close the second diamond — but not by declaring victory. Testing generates evidence about whether your solution actually works for the people it's meant to serve. The outcome is learning, not validation.

**The discipline:** You are investigating, not presenting. If your test can't produce a result that would make you change direction, it's not a test.

## Investigation, Not Validation

The difference matters:

| Validation mindset | Investigation mindset |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| "Let's show them our solution" | "Let's see what happens when they try it" |
| "Did they like it?" | "Could they use it? Where did they struggle?" |
| "It tested well" | "Here's what we learned and what we'd change" |
| Confirmation bias | Genuine inquiry |

**The tell:** If the team feels relieved after testing, they were validating. If they feel energised and slightly uncomfortable, they were investigating.

## Key Methods

**Think-aloud protocol:** Ask users to narrate their thinking while interacting with the prototype. "Tell me what you're looking at... what are you thinking right now... what would you do next?" Reveals cognitive process, not just outcomes.

**Observation over opinion:** Watch what users *do* with the prototype. Where do they hesitate? What do they skip? What do they try that you didn't anticipate? Behaviour is more reliable than stated preference.

**The 5-second test:** Show the prototype for 5 seconds, then remove it. "What was this for? What would you do first?" Tests clarity of communication, not usability.

**Structured feedback capture:**
- What worked? (Keep)
- What confused? (Change)
- What's missing? (Add)
- What surprised us? (Investigate)

## Iteration Triggers

Testing should produce clear signals about what to do next:

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Users can't identify what it's for | Return to Define — problem framing may be wrong |
| Users understand but can't complete the task | Iterate on Prototype — execution needs work |
| Users complete the task but find workarounds | Investigate the workaround — it may be the real solution |
| Users succeed easily and find value | Move toward implementation with confidence |

## Common Mistakes

- Testing with colleagues instead of actual users
- Explaining the prototype before letting users interact with it
- Treating "they liked it" as equivalent to "it works"
- Running one test round. Minimum two: test → iterate → retest
