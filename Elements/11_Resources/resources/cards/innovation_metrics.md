# Innovation Metrics: Stage-Appropriate Measurement

## Why It Matters

The single most damaging governance failure in innovation: applying mature-stage metrics (ROI, revenue, market share) to early-stage work (discovery, define). This creates selection pressure for incremental projects (predictable outcomes) over transformational ones (uncertain but high-potential).

## Key Principles

**Metrics Must Match Stage:** Measuring Discovery by revenue is like grading a baby on its running speed. Different stages require fundamentally different success indicators.

| Stage | Appropriate Metrics |
|-------|--------------------|
| **Discovery** | Number of interviews, depth of insight, users observed |
| **Define** | Problem clarity, stakeholder alignment, assumption inventory |
| **Ideate** | Quantity and diversity of concepts, breadth of exploration |
| **Prototype** | Speed to testable artifact, number of assumptions tested |
| **Test** | Evidence gathered, invalidation rate, quality of learning |
| **Scale** | Market fit indicators, adoption rates, unit economics |

**Metered Funding:** Allocate small budgets per stage. At each gate, assess learning evidence and decide continuation. Don't ask "what's the ROI?" at stage 1 — ask "have we learned enough to justify the next £5k?"

**Learning Velocity:** The most valuable early-stage metric is how quickly the team is reducing uncertainty through evidence-based learning.

## Practical Application

- **Agree metrics per stage** in the innovation charter — before work begins
- **Report learning, not just activity** — "We invalidated 3 assumptions" > "We held 6 workshops"
