# What is Foundations of Innovation Literacy Workshop Series?

## Definition

Innovation literacy is the foundational understanding of innovation as a structured, learnable process - and the capability to recognise, participate in, and support that process effectively. 

This Foundations workshop series is not about expertise or mastery; it is about developing sufficient understanding to continue your learning journey towards competence, ultimately enabling you to avoid cargo-culting, ask the right questions, recognise good practice, and make informed decisions about where and how to invest effort.

## Why It Matters

Innovation fails 70% of the time. The pattern is consistent: organisations build innovation management structures without process capability, or teach process skills without giving people the tools to defend them organisationally. You end up with innovation labs that can't innovate, or skilled practitioners who get crushed by operational pressure.

Most organisations possess partial innovation literacy - teams may excel at ideation but lack structured evaluation, or embrace experimentation without frameworks to capture and scale what works. Partial literacy creates blind spots that contribute to failure rates.

This framework addresses the complete picture: Elements 1-9 provide process-level capability (knowing how to innovate), and Element 10 provides the bridge to management-level capability (protecting the conditions that make innovation possible). Innovation literacy means having both.

## The Workshop Outcome

You'll learn innovation by doing it badly, then doing it well, then understanding why the difference matters - so you can spot cargo-culting, protect real innovation work from operational gravity, and build the capability in your organisation without the typical 70% failure rate.

By the end of this you will have learnt to recognise the key elements of innovation and have a sense of why they are important and when they come into play. To fully develop your innovation literacy you will have to embed and practise process-led innovation into your work.

Ultimately, this workshop series is about pointing to things, indicating where they sit in the framework, and contextualising through experiential learning:

- Not mastery - pointing, not deep-diving
- Not theoretical - showing where concepts live in practice, not explaining them in abstract
- Experience first - they feel it, then we name it, then we give them the map

## The Elements of Innovation Literacy

**The What (1-3):**
1. **Process** - Structured, repeatable activity
2. **Interventions** - Stage-dependent mini-processes
3. **Tools** - Technologies and platforms

**The How (4-9):**
4. **Skills and Competencies** - Analytical, creative, communication capabilities, systems thinking
5. **Mindset** - Cognitive orientations that enable innovation
6. **Behaviours** - Observable actions that support the work
7. **Environment** - Physical, temporal, and cultural conditions that protect innovation behaviours
8. **Collaboration** - Effective teamwork and creative collaboration dynamics
9. **Facilitation** - Enabling and guiding the process

**The Why (10-11):**
10. **Governance** - Bidirectional communication infrastructure mediating shared organisational purpose
11. **Resources** - Securing and defending time, budget, people, and materials in ongoing competition with operational priorities
