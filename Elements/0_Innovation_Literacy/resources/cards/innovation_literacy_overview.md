# What is Innovation Literacy?

The foundational understanding of innovation as a structured, learnable process — and the capability to recognise, participate in, and support that process effectively.

## Why It Matters

Innovation fails 70% of the time. The pattern is consistent: organisations build innovation management structures without process capability, or teach process skills without giving people the tools to defend them organisationally.

Most organisations possess partial innovation literacy — teams may excel at ideation but lack structured evaluation, or embrace experimentation without frameworks to capture and scale what works. Partial literacy creates blind spots that contribute to failure rates.

## The Eleven Elements

**The What (1-3):** Process, Interventions, Tools
**The How (4-9):** Skills & Competencies, Mindset, Behaviours, Environment, Collaboration, Facilitation
**The Why (10-11):** Governance, Resources

Elements 1-9 provide process-level capability (knowing how to innovate). Elements 10-11 provide the bridge to management-level capability (protecting the conditions that make innovation possible).

## The Workshop Outcome

You learn innovation by doing it badly, then doing it well, then understanding why the difference matters — so you can spot cargo-culting, protect real innovation work from operational gravity, and build capability without the typical 70% failure rate.
