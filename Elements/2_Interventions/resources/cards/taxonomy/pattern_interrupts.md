# Pattern Interrupts: Micro-Interventions That Change Behaviour

## Why They Matter

Pattern interrupts are single phrases or instructions that disrupt default behaviours in the moment they're about to occur. They appear trivial — just a few words — but they're the mechanism through which process actually changes behaviour.

Without them, teams know intellectually what good innovation looks like but default to habitual patterns under pressure.

## The Key Pattern Interrupts

| Interrupt | When | Effect |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **"Dig deeper"** | After first-level answers in user research | Prevents stopping at surface symptoms |
| **"Ask why five times"** | Problem analysis | Moves from symptoms to systemic causes |
| **"Don't explain, don't defend, don't evaluate"** | Divergent ideation | Protects generative thinking |
| **"Build on that"** | When someone's about to say "yes, but..." | Forces additive contribution |
| **"Show me, don't tell me"** | User research | Shifts from self-reported to observed behaviour |
| **"What's the riskiest assumption?"** | Prototyping | Focuses on highest-uncertainty elements |
| **"Don't defend your prototype"** | Before testing | Prevents rescuing prototypes during feedback |
| **"It should break"** | Over-polished prototyping | Permission for low-fidelity, disposable work |

## Why They Work

Applied at the exact moment behaviour is forming, before it solidifies. They're the tactical implementation layer beneath strategic process frameworks.

## Practical Application

- **Introduce as explicit rules:** Write them visibly — "Rules for this phase: Don't explain, don't defend, don't evaluate"
- **Intervene immediately when violated:** Gentle, consistent enforcement
- **Name why they matter:** Brief explanation builds buy-in
- **Model them in facilitation:** Use the language yourself
- **Rotate enforcement:** Designate team members to watch for violations
- **Debrief why they worked:** Make the mechanism visible post-session
