# What are Interventions?

## Definition

Interventions are deliberate, structured actions taken at specific points in the innovation process to shape, test, or redirect outcomes. They are pattern interrupts - instructions or frameworks applied at strategic moments to prevent common failure modes and move work forward more effectively.

Interventions are distinct from tools: tools are WHAT you use; interventions are HOW you're instructed to use them.

## Why It Matters

Process maps (like Design Thinking) show the stages. Tools provide the means. But without interventions, teams stall in discovery (surface-level inquiry), jump to solutions (weak problem framing), produce low-quality ideation (first acceptable idea wins), over-invest in prototypes (perfectionism), and extract no learning from testing (confirmation bias).

Interventions are the active ingredient that makes process work. They're small, deliberate, and stage-specific. "Dig deeper" is three words but changes the entire quality of discovery. "Don't defend your prototype" restructures testing from validation-seeking to learning-focused.

Every intervention is an act of de-risking: reducing uncertainty by generating evidence before committing resources.

## How It Shows Up in Practice

Interventions span the full Design Thinking process:

**Discovery:** "Dig deeper" prompt, "Ask 'why' five times", "Show me not tell me", silence discipline, empathy interview protocols

**Define:** How Might We statements, "Remove your solution from that problem statement", Five Whys, assumption mapping, problem space mapping

**Ideate:** "Don't explain, don't defend, don't evaluate", quantity targets ("50 ideas in 15 minutes"), "Build on that" not "but...", SCAMPER, worst possible idea

**Prototype:** "Build it in 7 minutes" (time constraint), "It should break" (permission for rough), "What's the riskiest assumption?" (focus prototype)

**Test:** "Don't defend your prototype", "Ask 'what did you notice?' not 'did you like it?'", think-aloud protocols, A/B testing frameworks

**Cross-stage:** 2x2 prioritisation matrices, stakeholder mapping, reflection protocols, change impact assessment (for technology adoption contexts)
