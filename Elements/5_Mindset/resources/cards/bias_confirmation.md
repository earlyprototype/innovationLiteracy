# Cognitive Biases: Confirmation, Sunk Cost & Availability

## Why It Matters

Human cognition is riddled with systematic biases that distort judgement under uncertainty. Understanding them doesn't eliminate them — it creates the possibility of designing processes that counteract them.

The solution isn't individual willpower — it's structural.

## Confirmation Bias

**Pattern:** Seeking, interpreting, and remembering information that confirms existing beliefs whilst ignoring contradictions.

**In Innovation:** Teams "test" prototypes to prove they work rather than to discover if they work.

**Counter-measure:** Structure testing with explicit success AND failure criteria defined before testing. Ask: "What would need to be true for us to abandon this idea?"

## Sunk Cost Fallacy

**Pattern:** Continuing investment because of resources already committed rather than future potential.

**In Innovation:** "We've already spent six months on this" justifies continuing rather than pivoting.

**Counter-measure:** Stage-gate governance with metered funding. At each gate, evaluate based solely on evidence and future potential — explicitly exclude past investment.

## Availability Bias

**Pattern:** Over-weighting easily recalled or recent information.

**In Innovation:** The last user interview, most memorable anecdote, or loudest voice dominates over systematic evidence.

**Counter-measure:** Document and synthesise ALL research systematically. Use structured prioritisation (2x2 matrices, weighted criteria) rather than "what feels important."
