# Growth Mindset & Navigating Ambiguity

## Why It Matters

The "groan zone" — the period of high ambiguity where the old way no longer works and the new solution isn't yet clear — destroys teams with fixed mindsets. Fixed mindset interprets ambiguity as incompetence. Growth mindset reframes it as discovery terrain.

Mindsets are not personality traits but learned patterns that can be deliberately cultivated.

## Key Principles

**Reframing Failure:** In operations, failure = missing a target. In innovation, failure = data acquisition strategy. Growth mindset separates failure of an *idea* from failure of the *person*.

**The "Not Yet" Principle:** "We don't have a solution yet" is fundamentally different from "we can't solve this." This linguistic shift sustains cognitive resilience.

**Ambiguity is a Feature:** Feeling lost during Empathise and Define is a *necessary condition*. If you know exactly what to do from day one, you're executing an operational plan, not innovating.

**Effort Creates Capability:** Growth mindset reframes effort from inadequacy ("if I was good at this, it wouldn't be hard") to development ("this is hard because I'm building new capability").

## Practical Application

- **Name the ambiguity:** "We are in the messy middle right now, and that means we are doing it right"
- **Celebrate invalidation:** "What valuable data did this test buy us?"
- **Track learning, not just outcomes:** Document what the team learned, not just produced
- **Model from leadership:** Leaders who ask questions rather than provide answers give teams permission
