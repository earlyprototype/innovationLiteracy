# Cognitive Biases: Groupthink & IKEA Effect

## Groupthink

**Pattern:** Cohesive groups converge on consensus whilst suppressing dissent. Teams value harmony over rigorous evaluation.

**In Innovation:** Produces poor decisions everyone superficially agrees with. Nobody challenges because challenge feels like disloyalty.

**Counter-measure:**
- Assign explicit "devil's advocate" role
- Use anonymous input methods (silent brainwriting, digital voting) before group discussion
- Celebrate constructive challenge as contribution, not disruption

## IKEA Effect

**Pattern:** Over-valuing things we've created ourselves. Teams fall in love with their own ideas and prototypes.

**In Innovation:** Objective evaluation becomes nearly impossible when creators are evaluating their own work.

**Counter-measure:**
- **"Don't defend your prototype"** intervention during testing
- Treat prototypes as disposable learning tools, not creations
- Rotate who tests what — don't let creators test their own work

## Structural Defence

Awareness alone isn't enough. Design processes that counteract biases structurally:

| Bias | Process Defence |
|------|----------------|
| Confirmation | Pre-defined success/failure criteria |
| Sunk Cost | Metered funding at stage gates |
| Availability | Systematic documentation + structured prioritisation |
| Groupthink | Anonymous input → then discussion |
| IKEA Effect | Creator-observer separation during testing |
