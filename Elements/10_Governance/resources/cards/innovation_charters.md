# Innovation Charters & Boundaries

## Why It Matters

Innovation and operations operate under opposed paradigms. Operations reduces variance; innovation explores it. Without explicit governance — a written agreement on the rules — operational gravity will always crush innovation work.

## The Innovation Charter

A formal agreement between innovation team and leadership defining:
- **Scope of exploration:** What territory is open
- **Protected resources:** Ring-fenced time and budget
- **Success metrics:** Learning-based (not ROI) in early stages

## Key Principles

**Shedding Authority at the Door:** When senior leaders enter innovation space (e.g., prototype review), they must act as "curious contributors" not "decision-makers." Traditional authority shuts down psychological safety.

**Temporal Ring-Fencing:** "Innovation on Friday afternoons" fails — it's the first thing sacrificed when deadlines loom. Governance must officially protect time from reallocation by middle management.

## Practical Application

- **Co-create your charter:** Before launching, develop with the project sponsor — explicitly agree what "productive failure" looks like
- **Ring-fence time:** Formalise agreement on how team time is protected from operational demands
- **Brief sponsors before reviews:** "Your role today is not to evaluate whether this makes money, but to ask questions that help the team see blind spots"
