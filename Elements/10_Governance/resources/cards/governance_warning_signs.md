# Governance Warning Signs

## Why It Matters

Governance failures don't announce themselves. They manifest as slow erosion — innovation time reclaimed, permission structures ignored, learning dismissed. Warning signs appear early; recognising them enables intervention before collapse.

## The Warning Signs

### 1. Innovation Time is "Borrowed"
**Symptom:** "We need you on this deadline — innovation can wait." Ring-fenced days postponed "just this once" repeatedly.
**Signal:** Innovation isn't genuinely protected. Operations dominates by default.

### 2. "Show Us Results" Too Early
**Symptom:** Business cases demanded during discovery. "When will this make money?" in week 2.
**Signal:** Decision-makers applying operational metrics to innovation work.

### 3. Failure is Punished
**Symptom:** Post-mortems become blame exercises. People who tested failed ideas viewed negatively.
**Signal:** Stated values ("we learn from failure") contradict lived experience.

### 4. Process Stages are Skipped
**Symptom:** Jumping from discovery to implementation. "We don't need more research."
**Signal:** Pressure to deliver overwhelms process discipline.

### 5. Innovation Theatre
**Symptom:** Visible activities (workshops, post-its) without genuine exploration. Leadership attends sessions but ignores outputs.
**Signal:** Innovation performed for appearance, not for genuine exploration.

### 6. Champion Burnout
**Symptom:** Innovation champion increasingly frustrated, isolated, doing all the advocating alone.
**Signal:** Governance support is rhetorical, not structural.
