# Governance Interventions

## What To Do When Warning Signs Appear

| Warning Sign | Intervention |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Time borrowed** | Escalate to sponsors. Make pattern visible with data. Reaffirm protection or acknowledge failure |
| **Results too early** | Re-educate sponsors on innovation accounting. Establish stage-appropriate metrics |
| **Failure punished** | Document contradiction between stated/lived values. Advocate for explicit safe-to-fail experiments |
| **Stages skipped** | Re-anchor to process framework. Present evidence for why stages matter |
| **Innovation theatre** | Measure genuine exploration, not activity volume. Challenge performative practices |
| **Champion burnout** | Distribute advocacy. Build structural support, don't rely on individual heroism |

## Structural Defences

- **Written charter** with explicit permissions and protections
- **Stage-gate governance** with learning-based metrics at each gate
- **Sponsor briefings** before every review session
- **Regular governance health checks** using warning sign checklist
- **Multiple champions** rather than single point of advocacy failure

## The Hard Truth

If governance interventions repeatedly fail, the honest response is to acknowledge that the organisation is not currently ready for innovation and stop pretending — rather than consuming people's energy in performative activity.
