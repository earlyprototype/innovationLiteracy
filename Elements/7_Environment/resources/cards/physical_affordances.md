# Physical Affordances & Making Space

## Core Principle

Space is the "third teacher" in innovation. The physical environment dictates cognitive behaviour. You cannot expect divergent, creative, collaborative behaviour in a boardroom designed for sequential, hierarchical reporting.

## Key Principles

**Posture Dictates Mindset:** Sitting behind laptops → defensive, operational thinking. Standing, moving, gathering at whiteboards → collaborative, exploratory thinking.

**Zoning for Innovation:** Different cognitive tasks need distinct affordances — collaborative areas for alignment, active spaces for ideation, quiet retreats for reflection. You can't freely ideate in a rigid boardroom.

**Modularity Grants Permission:** Overly polished spaces say "do not touch." Modular, rough, hackable spaces say "experiment."

**Visual Thinking:** If ideas only live in digital docs, they can't be passively absorbed. Wall space is a critical innovation tool.

**Sensory Regulation:** Varying acoustics, lighting, and visual barriers support cognitive diversity and neurodivergent thinking styles.

## Practical Application

- **Abandon the boardroom:** Never conduct innovation in a standard meeting room if avoidable
- **Hack the environment:** Move tables to edges, remove chairs, cover walls in paper
- **Design for duality:** Active/loud areas for divergence AND quiet/focused areas for convergence
