# Defining Team Purpose

## Why It Matters
Effective collaboration is not just about bringing diverse people together; it requires a shared understanding of *why* they are together. A clearly defined purpose acts as the North Star for an innovation team. It provides alignment, sustains momentum through periods of ambiguity, and serves as the ultimate tie-breaker during difficult convergent decision-making phases.

## Key Takeaways
- **Alignment Over Agreement:** Teams do not need to agree on every detail, but they must be aligned on the overarching purpose of their work.
- **Intrinsic Motivation:** A strong purpose taps into intrinsic motivation, which is essential for sustaining the cognitive load and resilience required in innovation.
- **Clarity of Focus:** By explicitly defining what the team is trying to achieve, it becomes much easier to identify and discard out-of-scope distractions.

## Practical Application Tips
- **Run a purpose-setting session:** Before diving into the mechanics of a new innovation project, dedicate time specifically to defining the team's purpose, ensuring every member has a voice.
- **Make it highly visible:** Document the agreed-upon purpose and display it prominently in your physical or digital workspace (Environment).
- **Use it as a tie-breaker:** When the team experiences friction or gets lost in the weeds, continually reference the written purpose to re-align the group's focus.
