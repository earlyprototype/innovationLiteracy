# Practical Exercise: The User Manual of Me

**Source:** Cassie Robinson (Cassie Robinson's User Manual)

## Why It Matters
Cognitive diversity is the raw material of innovation, but it naturally produces friction. People who think differently also tend to communicate, process information, and handle stress differently. If team members don't understand *how* their colleagues operate, this necessary cognitive friction devolves into interpersonal conflict, destroying psychological safety.

## Key Takeaways
- **Proactive Empathy:** The exercise forces team members to empathise with each other *before* the work gets stressful.
- **Operationalising Neurodiversity:** It provides a structured way for neurodivergent or introverted team members to state their needs (e.g., "I need 10 minutes of silence after a brainstorming session to process before I can vote") without having to awkwardly ask for accommodations in the heat of the moment.
- **Clarifying Communication:** It removes the guesswork from collaboration by making implicit personal boundaries explicit.

## Practical Application Tips
- **Pre-work:** Before a long-term project begins, have each team member fill out a simple one-page template.
- **The Prompts:** Include questions like: 
  - "The best way to communicate with me is..."
  - "The times/situations where I do my best work are..."
  - "I get stressed when..."
  - "How to tell if I'm stressed..."
  - "The best way to give me feedback is..."
- **The Review:** Spend the first 30 minutes of your kickoff meeting having the team read through each other's manuals. Keep them pinned to the wall (or the top of your digital workspace) as a persistent environmental reminder of the team's diverse working styles.
