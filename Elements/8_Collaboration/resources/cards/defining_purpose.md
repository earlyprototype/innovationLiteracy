# Defining Team Purpose

## Why It Matters

Effective collaboration isn't just about bringing diverse people together — it requires shared understanding of *why* they are together. Purpose acts as the North Star: providing alignment, sustaining momentum through ambiguity, and serving as the ultimate tie-breaker during difficult decisions.

## Key Principles

**Alignment Over Agreement:** Teams don't need to agree on every detail, but they must be aligned on overarching purpose.

**Intrinsic Motivation:** Strong purpose taps into intrinsic motivation — essential for sustaining cognitive load and resilience required in innovation.

**Clarity of Focus:** Explicit purpose makes it easier to identify and discard out-of-scope distractions.

## Practical Application

- **Run a purpose-setting session:** Before diving into mechanics, dedicate time specifically to defining team purpose — every member gets a voice
- **Make it visible:** Document and display prominently in physical or digital workspace
- **Use as tie-breaker:** When friction arises or the team gets lost, reference the purpose to re-align
