# Exercise: The Stinky Fish

**Source:** Hyper Island / SessionLab

## Why It Matters

You cannot simply tell a newly formed team to "have psychological safety." You have to manufacture the conditions. The Stinky Fish exercise forces hidden anxieties into the open immediately, demonstrating the environment is safe for vulnerability.

## How It Works

A "stinky fish" is something you carry around but are afraid to talk about — the longer you hide it, the worse it smells. Once placed on the table, it loses power.

## Running the Exercise

1. **Timing:** Project kickoff or start of Define phase
2. **Prompt:** Each person writes their stinky fish on a sticky note
   - "I'm worried we don't actually have budget for this"
   - "I don't understand the technology we're supposed to be using"
   - "I think leadership doesn't really support this"
3. **Share without solving:** Everyone places notes on board and briefly explains
4. **Facilitator rule:** Do NOT try to solve problems in the room — the goal is acknowledgement

## Key Effects

- **Normalises discomfort:** Scheduling time to air fears signals discomfort is expected
- **Defuses anxiety:** Named fears lose their power to silently disrupt
- **Levels hierarchy:** Senior leaders sharing genuine anxieties gives juniors permission to speak
