# Practical Exercise: The "Stinky Fish"

**Source:** Hyper Island / SessionLab

## Why It Matters
You cannot simply tell a newly formed innovation team to "have psychological safety." You have to manufacture the conditions for it. When groups form to tackle complex, ambiguous problems, individuals naturally carry unvoiced fears, anxieties, or hidden agendas. If left unspoken, these concerns act as a drag on collaboration. The "Stinky Fish" exercise forces these hidden anxieties into the open immediately, demonstrating that the environment is safe for vulnerability.

## Key Takeaways
- **Normalising Discomfort:** By formally scheduling a time to air grievances or fears, you signal that discomfort is a normal, expected part of the innovation process.
- **Defusing Anxiety:** A "stinky fish" is something you carry around but are afraid to talk about; the longer you hide it, the worse it smells. Once it is placed on the table, it loses its power to silently disrupt the team's cognitive diversity.
- **Levelling the Hierarchy:** When senior leaders participate and share genuine anxieties about a project's feasibility or their own lack of technical knowledge, it instantly flattens the hierarchy and gives junior members permission to speak freely.

## Practical Application Tips
- **Run this early:** Conduct this exercise during the project kickoff or at the very start of the Define phase.
- **The prompt:** Ask each team member to write down their "stinky fish" on a sticky note (e.g., "I am worried we don't actually have budget for this," or "I don't understand the technology we are supposed to be using").
- **Share without solving:** Have everyone place their notes on a board and briefly explain them. Crucially, as a facilitator, *do not try to solve the problems in the room*. The goal is simply to acknowledge them. Just making the hidden visible builds the trust required for the rest of the process.
