# WEF Future of Jobs and Innovation Skills

## Why It Matters
The World Economic Forum's (WEF) Future of Jobs Report consistently highlights that the core skills required for the modern workplace are no longer rote execution or manual operational tasks. The taxonomy of top skills (Analytical Thinking, Creative Thinking, Resilience, Flexibility, and Complex Problem-Solving) maps almost perfectly one-to-one with the foundational competencies required for effective innovation. Innovation literacy is therefore synonymous with future-proofing a workforce.

## Key Takeaways
- **Convergence of Skills:** The skills needed to run a dedicated innovation process are the exact same skills required to survive in a rapidly changing operational landscape. 
- **Beyond Technical Expertise:** The WEF taxonomy prioritises cognitive and behavioural competencies (how you think and adapt) over purely technical or domain-specific knowledge (what you currently know).
- **Learnable Competencies:** These are not innate personality traits. Analytical thinking and creative problem-solving are capabilities that can be actively developed through the structured practice of innovation.

## Practical Application Tips
- **Reframe innovation training:** When securing resources or governance for innovation literacy, frame the investment as essential workforce readiness and resilience training, citing the WEF data.
- **Embed skills in daily work:** Use innovation processes (like empathy mapping or structured ideation) as a Trojan horse to develop these WEF-identified competencies within your operational teams.
