# WEF Future of Jobs & Innovation Skills

## Why It Matters

The World Economic Forum's Future of Jobs Report highlights that core skills for the modern workplace — Analytical Thinking, Creative Thinking, Resilience, Flexibility, Complex Problem-Solving — map almost perfectly to the competencies required for effective innovation.

Innovation literacy is therefore synonymous with future-proofing a workforce.

## Key Takeaways

**Convergence of Skills:** The skills needed to run a dedicated innovation process are the exact same skills required to survive in a rapidly changing operational landscape.

**Beyond Technical Expertise:** The WEF taxonomy prioritises cognitive and behavioural competencies (how you think and adapt) over purely technical or domain-specific knowledge (what you currently know).

**Learnable Competencies:** These are not innate personality traits. Analytical thinking and creative problem-solving are capabilities that can be actively developed through the structured practice of innovation.

## Practical Application

- **Reframe innovation training:** Frame investment as essential workforce readiness and resilience training, citing WEF data
- **Embed skills in daily work:** Use innovation processes (empathy mapping, structured ideation) as a Trojan horse to develop WEF-identified competencies within operational teams
