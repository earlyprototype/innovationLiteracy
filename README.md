# Fundamentals of Innovation Literacy - Workshop Materials

This repository contains the complete framework and supporting materials for the **Fundamentals of Innovation Literacy** workshop series.

## What This Is

Innovation literacy is the foundational understanding of innovation as a structured, learnable process - and the capability to recognise, participate in, and support that process effectively.

This workshop series addresses the complete picture: Elements 1-9 provide process-level capability (knowing how to innovate), and Elements 10-11 provide the organisational capability (protecting the conditions that make innovation possible). Innovation literacy means having both.

## Framework Structure

The framework consists of **11 Elements** organised into three groups:

### The What (Elements 1-3)
1. **Process** - Structured, repeatable activity
2. **Interventions** - Stage-dependent methods
3. **Tools** - Technologies and platforms

### The How (Elements 4-9)
4. **Skills and Competencies** - Analytical, creative, systems thinking capabilities
5. **Mindset** - Cognitive orientations that enable innovation
6. **Behaviours** - Observable actions that support the work
7. **Environment** - Conditions that protect innovation from operational gravity
8. **Collaboration** - Effective teamwork and creative dynamics
9. **Facilitation** - Guiding process and unlocking potential

### The Why (Elements 10-11)
10. **Governance** - Bidirectional communication infrastructure
11. **Resources** - Securing time, budget, people, materials

## Key Distinctions in This Framework

**Tools vs Interventions:**
- Tools = WHAT you use (Miro, sticky notes, AI assistants)
- Interventions = HOW you're instructed to use them ("dig deeper", "don't defend your prototype")

**Environment vs Governance:**
- Environment = The container (physical spaces, temporal boundaries, cultural conditions)
- Governance = The legitimising structure (bidirectional communication, formal mechanisms)

**Innovation Literacy vs Innovation Expertise:**
- Literacy = Foundational understanding, ability to recognise and participate
- Expertise = Deep capability, ability to design and lead innovation practice
- This workshop targets literacy, creating foundation for expertise development
