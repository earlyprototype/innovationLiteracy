## Pedagogical Foundation

**What this isn't:** Lecture-based information transfer. No discussion prompts. No presentation of theory followed by application exercises.

**What this is:** Experiential learning where theory is handed off AFTER participants have felt the need for it.

**Method:**
- IDEO micro-cycles (Wallet Project in Week 1, Gift-Giving Project in Week 2)
- Trivial challenges keep cognitive load low - focus on process, not content
- Tight timing creates productive pressure (this is deliberate, not accidental)
- Structured reflection extracts learning from experience
- Framework handoffs at strategic moments: "Here's the map for what you just experienced"

**Facilitation stance:** Point to things, indicate where they sit in the framework, contextualise through practice. Not expert-presenter. Provocateur-guide managing the container.

**Learning outcome:** Foundational literacy, not mastery. Baseline understanding sufficient to recognise the landscape, know what matters, and continue building capability through practice.

## The 11 Elements

**The What (1-3):** What innovation requires as activity
- Process, Interventions, Tools

**The How (4-9):** How it's executed at individual and collective levels
- Skills, Mindset, Behaviours, Environment, Collaboration, Facilitation

**The Why (10-11):** Why organisations support it and what that requires
- Governance, Resources

All 11 elements comprise Innovation Literacy. Participants experience all elements throughout both weeks.
