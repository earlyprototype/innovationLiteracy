# What is Process?

## Definition

Process refers to a structured set of steps or boundaries that are useful for orientation during the larger innovation process. 

An interesting element of thet processes that help structure innovation is that, although they do indicate sequence they are not necessarily linear and are gatted rather than time bound (although this might be a real limitation in practice). Often failure to pass through a gate to the next process step indicates a need to return to an earlier step, thus creating an interative, looping process that incrementally drives towards high quality outputs. This has the additional effect of removing the concept of 'failure' from the process, replacing it with a new, better informed process loop.

## Why It Matters

Engaging in process-led innovation enables:
 - Team alignment
 - Coherence and efficiency
 - Expectation setting
 - The ability to integrate appropriate interventions to support the process
 - Avoiding putting the horse before the cart (or solutions before problems, for example)
