## Slide Title
1.2 Interventions -- Deliberate Actions at Key Points

### Text
An **intervention** is a deliberate, structured action designed to answer a specific question at a specific point in the process.

#### Across the Process:
- **Problem Framing** -- empathy maps, "How Might We" questions, root cause analysis
- **Opportunity Identification** -- environmental scanning, constraint mapping, analogous domain exploration
- **Ideation** -- structured brainstorming, brainwriting, provocation techniques
- **Prototyping & Validation** -- rapid prototyping, "try before you test," staged commitment
- **Evaluation** -- decision matrices, pre-mortem analysis, learning capture

#### The Principle of De-risking
Every intervention reduces uncertainty by generating evidence before committing resources. The earlier interventions occur, the less expensive they are.

### Speaker Notes
The key distinction to land: an intervention is not just an activity -- it is an activity designed to answer a specific question. Reframing activities as interventions (by clarifying what question they answer) significantly increases their value. The de-risking principle connects directly to Eirmersive's "try before you test" programme model -- they already practise staged de-risking; this section gives them the broader framework. Walk through one or two intervention types with concrete examples. The staged commitment model is particularly relevant: rather than one large investment decision, resources are committed incrementally as evidence accumulates.
