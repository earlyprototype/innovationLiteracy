## Slide Title
Active Listening -- A Foundational Innovation Behaviour

### Text
Active listening is not simply hearing. It is the deliberate practice of receiving and integrating another person's perspective before responding.

#### Why It Matters at Every Stage
- **Empathise**: insight quality depends entirely on listening quality
- **Ideate**: building on ideas requires understanding them first
- **Test**: feedback is only useful if genuinely heard (not filtered by confirmation bias)
- **Teams**: active listening is the behavioural foundation of psychological safety

#### The Practice
- Full attention -- not formulating a response while the other person speaks
- Clarifying questions -- confirm understanding before building or responding
- Paraphrasing -- reflect back what was heard
- Suspending judgement -- receive fully before evaluating
- Attending to non-verbal cues

### Speaker Notes
This is worth its own slide because active listening is consistently underestimated. Most people believe they are good listeners; research consistently shows otherwise. Make it concrete: ask for a show of hands -- "how often do you catch yourself formulating your response while someone is still speaking?" The five practice elements give participants something actionable. Connect this to the earlier discussion of the lone innovator myth: if innovation depends on integrating diverse perspectives, the quality of listening determines the quality of that integration. This is not a soft skill; it is a technical capability with direct impact on innovation outcomes.
