## Slide Title
1.4 Skills and Competencies

### Text
Innovation depends on **identifiable, specific, learnable** skills -- not indefinable talent.

#### Core Innovation Skills
- **Analytical thinking** -- breaking complexity, identifying patterns, evaluating evidence
- **Creative thinking** -- novel ideas, unexpected connections, constrained creativity
- **Communication** -- articulating ideas, visual thinking, adapting to audience
- **Experimentation** -- designing tests, gathering evidence, comfort with disproof
- **Comfort with ambiguity** -- functioning when outcomes are uncertain
- **Collaboration** -- working across disciplines, sharing unfinished thinking
- **Critical evaluation** -- rigorous, constructive assessment of ideas and evidence

#### WEF Future of Jobs: Fastest-Growing Skills
Analytical thinking, creative thinking, resilience, flexibility, curiosity, lifelong learning, technological literacy, empathy, active listening

**Innovation literacy and workforce readiness are converging.**

### Speaker Notes
The critical shift here: innovation skills are not innate gifts, they are identifiable capabilities that can be developed through deliberate practice. This is empowering and should be framed as such. The WEF data provides external authority and connects innovation literacy to the broader workforce conversation. For Eirmersive specifically, these skills directly support their R&D and technology adoption work -- investing in innovation skills is simultaneously investing in core operational capability. Ask participants to self-assess: which skills feel strongest, and where are the development areas?
