## Slide Title
1.6 Behaviours -- From Mindset to Action

### Text
Mindset without behaviour is aspiration. Behaviour without mindset is performance.

#### Core Innovation Behaviours
- **Active Listening** -- receiving, processing, and integrating before responding
- **Constructive Challenge** -- questioning that strengthens, not undermines
- **Disciplined Experimentation** -- "let's test it" rather than "let's discuss it further"
- **Reflective Practice** -- pausing to extract learning from experience
- **Sharing Work in Progress** -- making thinking visible before it is "ready"
- **Building on Others' Ideas** -- "Yes, and..." rather than "No, but..."

**These are team behaviours as much as individual ones.**
They are most effective when established as explicit norms, not left to individual initiative.

### Speaker Notes
The opening line is the key framing: this section bridges 1.5 (Mindset) and the practical world. Each behaviour should be briefly illustrated with a concrete example. Active listening deserves extra attention -- it is not "being polite," it is a foundational innovation behaviour that determines the quality of empathy, ideation, evaluation, and team dynamics. The "sharing work in progress" point often generates strong reactions: many people are uncomfortable showing unfinished thinking. Acknowledge this, and connect it to psychological safety (1.7). The closing point about team norms vs individual initiative sets up the next slide.
