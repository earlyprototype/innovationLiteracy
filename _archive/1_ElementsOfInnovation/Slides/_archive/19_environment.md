## Slide Title
1.9 Environment -- Creating the Conditions

### Text
#### The Contradiction

| Innovation Requires | Operations Requires |
|---|---|
| Experimentation | Predictability |
| Quantity over quality | Consistency |
| Bias towards action | Thorough analysis |
| Tolerance for failure | Risk avoidance |
| Exploration | Exploitation |

These cannot be resolved through willpower. They require **deliberate boundaries**.

#### Three Types of Boundary
1. **Temporal** -- dedicated, protected time (not "innovation in your spare time")
2. **Physical/Virtual** -- distinct spaces that signal different norms
3. **Permission** -- explicit agreements about what is sanctioned

Without boundaries, operational culture invariably wins.

### Speaker Notes
The contradiction table is the centrepiece of this slide -- let participants read it and react. Most will immediately recognise the tension. The key insight: this is not a failure of organisational design, it is inherent in the nature of the two activities. The solution is not to choose one over the other but to create boundaries that allow both to coexist. "Innovation in your spare time" is a line that tends to get a knowing reaction -- it names a common failure pattern directly. The three boundary types give participants a practical framework for thinking about what their organisation needs. The permission boundaries point connects directly to Section 2 (the innovation charter). The closing line -- "operational culture invariably wins" -- should land as a statement of structural reality, not pessimism.
