--- CONTENT

## Slide Title
1.3 Tools -- Enablers, Not Solutions

### Text
Tools serve the process. The process does not serve the tools.

#### The Innovation Toolkit
| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Strategic Analysis | SWOT, PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, Business Model Canvas |
| Problem Framing | Empathy Maps, "How Might We," 5 Whys, Stakeholder Maps |
| Ideation | Brainstorming (structured), Brainwriting, SCAMPER, Affinity Mapping |
| Evaluation | Impact/Effort Matrix, Decision Matrix, Assumption Mapping, Pre-mortem |
| Digital & Emerging | Collaborative platforms (Miro, Mural), AI-assisted tools, Digital prototyping |

#### Selection Principles
1. Purpose first -- what do we need to learn or decide?
2. Simplicity -- use the simplest tool that works
3. Accessibility -- usable by all participants
4. Integration -- connects to the broader process

### Speaker Notes
This slide is a survey, not a deep dive -- the purpose is to show the breadth of what is available and to establish the selection principles. Resist the temptation to explain every tool; instead, offer to go deeper on any that are unfamiliar during discussion. The opening line is the most important: tools adopted without process understanding is cargo culting. The AI tools point is worth a brief note -- position AI as an emerging enabler with real potential, but one whose value depends entirely on the literacy of the people using it. The same critical thinking applies to AI outputs as to any other tool output.
