## Slide Title
1.7 Teams -- Innovation Is Collaborative

### Text
How people work together is as important as individual capability.

#### Four Characteristics of Effective Innovation Teams

1. **Psychological Safety**
   The shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. People contribute freely when they believe they will be heard -- not punished.
   *Google's Project Aristotle: the single most important factor in team effectiveness.*

2. **Cognitive Diversity**
   Different mental models, disciplinary backgrounds, and problem-solving approaches. The raw material of innovation -- but produces friction that must be managed.

3. **Role Clarity**
   Clarity about purpose and contribution ("why am I here, what do I bring") -- not rigid task assignment.

4. **The Expertise/Fresh Perspective Tension**
   Deep domain knowledge + outsider naivety = the most productive combination. Deliberately include both.

### Speaker Notes
This slide connects directly to the lone innovator myth (0.2) and the behaviours discussion (1.6). Psychological safety is the foundational condition -- spend the most time here. The Google Project Aristotle reference provides external authority: after studying hundreds of teams, Google found that who was on the team mattered less than how they worked together, and psychological safety was the top factor. Cognitive diversity is powerful but uncomfortable -- people who think differently will disagree and misunderstand each other. This friction is productive only when managed through the behaviours and facilitation discussed elsewhere. The expertise/fresh perspective tension is immediately practical: when composing a team, deliberately include both insiders and outsiders.
