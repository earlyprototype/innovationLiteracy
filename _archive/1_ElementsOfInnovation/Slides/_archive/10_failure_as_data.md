## Slide Title
Failure Is Data

### Text
Design Thinking is fundamentally **iterative**.

- Ideas will be tested and will often fall short
- Each iteration narrows uncertainty and builds evidence
- An experiment that disproves a hypothesis has produced valuable information

#### The Discipline
Every iteration must capture:
- What was tested
- What was observed
- What was concluded
- What changed as a result

#### Measurement
Evaluate by what is **done**, what is **captured**, and what is **learned** -- not solely by outputs.

Applying operational metrics to exploratory work is a form of cargo culting.

### Speaker Notes
This slide connects directly to the cargo culting discussion from Section 0. The "failure is data" framing is not a motivational platitude -- it is a structural principle. Make it concrete: if an experiment disproves a hypothesis, the team now knows something it did not know before. That knowledge has value. The measurement point is critical for Eirmersive's context: as an R&D-focused organisation, they need frameworks for demonstrating the value of work that has not yet produced commercial outputs. The four-point capture discipline (tested, observed, concluded, changed) gives them a practical tool.
