## Slide Title
1.8 Facilitation -- The Missing Ingredient

### Text
Facilitation is not a support function. It is a critical enabler.

#### What Facilitation Does
- Surfaces diverse perspectives (not just the loudest)
- Manages constructive conflict
- Holds the process -- divergent when diverging, convergent when converging
- Captures learning in real time
- Builds team capability through guided practice

#### The Facilitation Gap
Organisations invest in processes, tools, and training -- then expect groups to self-facilitate.

**Consequences:**
- Dominant voices prevail
- Premature convergence on "good enough"
- Undocumented outcomes forgotten within days
- Conflict avoided rather than managed

#### Different Stages Need Different Facilitation
- Empathise/Define: inquiry and synthesis
- Ideate: energy, volume, deferred judgement
- Prototype/Test: structure, feedback capture
- Evaluation: rigour and convergence

### Speaker Notes
The "missing ingredient" framing is deliberate -- facilitation is consistently the most undervalued and under-resourced element. The facilitation gap consequences will be immediately recognisable to most participants. Make the point that facilitation skill can be developed within teams; it does not always require an external specialist. But it does require recognition as a distinct skill and explicit allocation of the role. The stage-specific facilitation point is practical: the same person facilitating ideation needs a completely different approach when facilitating evaluation. This reinforces the broader point that innovation is a structured, differentiated process, not a single mode of working.
