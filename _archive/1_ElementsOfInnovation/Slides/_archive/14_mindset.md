## Slide Title
1.5 Mindset -- Cultivable, Not Fixed

### Text
Mindset is not personality. It is shaped by experience, environment, and deliberate practice.

#### Six Key Innovation Mindsets
1. **Curiosity** -- genuine orientation towards understanding ("why?" and "what if?")
2. **Tolerance for uncertainty** -- remaining productive when outcomes are unknown
3. **Willingness to challenge assumptions** -- questioning established beliefs, including one's own
4. **Perspective-taking** -- holding multiple viewpoints simultaneously
5. **Growth orientation** -- belief that capabilities develop through effort
6. **Bias towards action** -- learning through doing, not only analysing

#### The Critical Insight
The same person may exhibit different mindsets in different contexts. Someone who is curious and exploratory at home may become risk-averse at work.

**The question is not "do our people have the right mindset?" but "does our environment support the right mindset?"**

### Speaker Notes
The "cultivable, not fixed" framing is essential -- it shifts the conversation from "we need different people" to "we need different conditions." The context-dependency point is powerful: most people will recognise the experience of being more open and experimental outside work than within it. This is not a personal failing; it is a rational response to an environment that rewards certainty and penalises exploration. This directly sets up the Environment section (1.9). The six mindsets should be walked through with brief examples or questions that make each one concrete.
