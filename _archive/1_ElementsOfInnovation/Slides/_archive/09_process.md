## Slide Title
1.1 Process -- Innovation as a Structured Activity

### Text
Innovation is a **process**, not an event. It can be learned, practised, and improved.

#### Design Thinking: Five Phases
1. **Empathise** -- understand the people affected
2. **Define** -- frame the problem clearly
3. **Ideate** -- generate a wide range of possibilities
4. **Prototype** -- make thinking tangible and testable
5. **Test** -- learn from real feedback

#### Key Characteristics
- Non-linear (phases revisited as understanding deepens)
- Alternates between divergent and convergent thinking
- Human-centred throughout

### Speaker Notes
This is a substantial slide -- take time with it. The five phases should be walked through with brief, concrete examples. Emphasise that the phases are not a strict linear sequence; teams move back and forth as they learn. The divergent/convergent distinction is critical and often misunderstood: many teams try to evaluate ideas during ideation (convergent behaviour in a divergent phase), which kills creativity. Make the human-centred point concrete: the process starts with people, not with technology or organisational convenience.
