# 0.1 What Is Innovation?

## Purpose
 - 
 - Establish a shared, precise definition of innovation and distinguish it from commonly conflated concepts. 
 - Introduce the critical distinction between innovation as a process and innovation management as an organisational capability.

## Key Learning Points

### Defining Innovation
- Innovation is the deliberate creation of novel value -- not simply newness, but newness that produces a meaningful, measurable difference
- It is distinct from:
  - **Invention** -- the creation of something new, which may or may not produce value
  - **Improvement** -- incremental enhancement of existing processes or products, which may not involve novelty
  - **Change** -- any alteration to the status quo, which may be neither novel nor value-creating
- The "value" in innovation can take many forms: commercial, operational, strategic, social, or experiential
- Innovation is not inherently radical -- it spans a spectrum from incremental to transformative

### Process vs Management

#### Innovation as a Process
- A structured, repeatable, learnable sequence of activities that moves from problem or opportunity identification through to validated outcomes
- It can be taught, practised, and improved -- it is not a gift or a personality trait
- Design Thinking is one of the most widely adopted frameworks (explored in depth in Section 1.1)
- The process is not linear: it involves cycles of divergence (expanding possibilities) and convergence (narrowing to decisions)

#### Innovation Management
- The organisational capability to direct, resource, sustain, and govern innovation processes over time
- Encompasses: strategy and portfolio decisions, resource allocation, governance structures, culture and incentives, measurement and evaluation
- An organisation can have excellent innovation processes but poor innovation management (individual projects succeed but there is no systemic capability) -- or vice versa
- Both are required for sustained innovation; neither is sufficient alone

### Why This Distinction Matters
- Organisations frequently invest in process (workshops, tools, methodologies) without addressing management (time, permission, governance, measurement) -- and then wonder why innovation does not take root
- Conversely, some organisations build elaborate innovation management structures (labs, committees, budgets) without equipping people with the process skills to actually innovate
- Understanding both layers -- and where one's own organisation sits on each -- is the starting point for meaningful improvement

## Discussion Prompts
- When you hear the word "innovation," what comes to mind first? How does that compare to the definition we have established?
- In your organisation, where does innovation sit? Is it a managed capability, an ad hoc activity, or something in between?
- Can you identify examples of innovation process being present but innovation management being absent -- or the reverse?

## Key Takeaways
1. Innovation is the deliberate creation of novel value -- not invention, not improvement, not change for its own sake
2. Innovation operates at two levels: process (the doing) and management (the sustaining)
3. Most organisations have gaps in one or both -- identifying where those gaps lie is the first step
