# Fundamentals of Innovation Literacy - Slide 5

--- CONTENT

## Slide Title
Cargo Culting -- Form Without Substance

### Text
Post-WWII Pacific islands built replica airstrips expecting planes to land.

#### In Innovation:
- Post-it notes on walls without structured problem framing
- Innovation labs without governance or strategic connection
- Hackathons without follow-through mechanisms
- Design Thinking workshops without iterative implementation
- Innovation titles without authority, resources, or mandate

**The antidote is literacy**: understanding not just *what* innovative organisations do, but *why* those practices work and *what conditions* they require.

### Speaker Notes
Define cargo culting clearly -- the anthropological origin is vivid and memorable. Then make it concrete with the innovation examples. Most participants will recognise at least one of these patterns from their own experience. Emphasise that cargo culting is rarely cynical -- it usually arises from genuine intent combined with insufficient understanding. This is not about blaming anyone; it is about recognising the pattern so it can be avoided. The key point: the antidote is not more tools or more enthusiasm, it is literacy -- understanding the full picture.
