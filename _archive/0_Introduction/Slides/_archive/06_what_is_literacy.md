## Slide Title
0.3 What Do We Mean by 'Literacy'?

### Text
#### Literacy, Not Expertise
Financial literacy: read a balance sheet, ask informed questions, make sound decisions -- without being an accountant.

**Innovation literacy**: understand the landscape, participate effectively, recognise good practice, make informed decisions -- without being an innovation specialist.

#### What It Enables
- Informed participation in innovation processes
- Quality questioning at each stage
- Recognition of good practice (vs cargo culting)
- Sound resource allocation decisions
- Constructive challenge of weak thinking

#### What It Is Not
- A credential or certification
- A substitute for domain expertise
- A one-time acquisition

### Speaker Notes
The financial literacy analogy is powerful because almost everyone understands it intuitively. Use it to make the concept concrete. Emphasise the "quality questioning" point -- one of the most valuable outputs of innovation literacy is knowing what questions to ask, not having all the answers. Acknowledge that participants will sit at different points on the literacy spectrum, and that is expected. The goal is not to bring everyone to the same level but to ensure everyone has a complete enough picture to participate effectively.
