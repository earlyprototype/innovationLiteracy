## Slide Title
The Myth of the Lone Innovator

### Text
Edison ran a laboratory of dozens. The Macintosh was built by hundreds. SpaceX employs thousands of engineers.

#### Why this myth is harmful:
- Concentrates expectation on individuals, not systems
- Discourages participation ("innovation is for special people")
- Undermines the diversity of perspective that drives real innovation
- Creates single points of failure

**Innovation is a team-led process** that depends on diverse perspectives, skills, and expertise working together.

### Speaker Notes
This slide directly sets up the Teams section (1.7) later in the workshop. The examples are chosen to be recognisable -- most people know Edison, Jobs, Musk as "lone innovator" figures. The point is not to diminish their contributions but to correct the narrative: these were orchestrators and leaders of large, diverse teams. The practical consequence for any organisation: stop asking "who is our innovator?" and start asking "how do we innovate together?" This is a mindset shift that underpins everything that follows.
