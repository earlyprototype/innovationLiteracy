## Slide Title
0.2 Why Does Innovation Fail?

### Text
~70% of innovation initiatives fail to deliver intended outcomes

#### Three Root Causes
1. **Innovation is hard** -- inherently uncertain, structurally at odds with how organisations operate
2. **Cargo Culting** -- adopting innovation practices in form without substance
3. **The Myth of the Lone Innovator** -- the most damaging misconception in innovation

### Speaker Notes
This is a provocative slide -- let it land. The 70% figure is well-evidenced (McKinsey, BCG, multiple industry studies) and tends to get attention. The three root causes are deliberately ordered: difficulty is acknowledged first (validating their experience), then cargo culting names a pattern most will recognise but may not have had language for, then the lone innovator myth reframes expectations for the rest of the workshop. Do not rush past the 70% -- let participants react and share their own experiences of failed initiatives before moving to the causes.
