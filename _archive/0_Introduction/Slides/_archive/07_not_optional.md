## Slide Title
0.4 Why Innovation Literacy Is Not Optional

### Text
#### You Already Have Some of This
Most professionals already practise elements of innovation literacy -- through experience, instinct, or informal learning.

#### The Problem with Partial Literacy
Partial literacy creates blind spots:
- Strong ideation, weak evaluation
- Experimentation without learning capture
- Leadership support without operational protection
- Process skills without team behaviours

**Partial literacy is not neutral -- it can be actively harmful.**

#### The Global Context
WEF Future of Jobs: analytical thinking, creative thinking, resilience, curiosity, lifelong learning -- among the fastest-growing competency requirements across all industries.

### Speaker Notes
This slide does two things at once: it validates what participants already bring (important for engagement and credibility) and it raises the stakes by framing partial literacy as a liability. The blind spots examples should feel recognisable. The WEF data provides external authority -- this is not just one workshop provider's opinion, it is a global trend. Close this section by framing the rest of the workshop as completing the picture: developing what is latent, filling what is absent, making it deliberate.
