# Fundamentals of Innovation Literacy - Slide 3

## Slide Title
0.1 What Is Innovation?

### Text
#### Innovation is the deliberate creation of novel value

It is NOT:
- Invention (newness without value)
- Improvement (enhancement without novelty)
- Change (alteration without either)

#### Two Levels
- **Innovation Process** -- structured, repeatable, learnable activity
- **Innovation Management** -- organisational capability to direct, resource, and sustain that process

Both are required. Neither is sufficient alone.

### Speaker Notes
Open with a question to the room: "When you hear the word innovation, what comes to mind?" Allow 2-3 responses. Use these to anchor the distinction between the popular understanding and the working definition. The process vs management distinction is foundational -- return to it throughout. Many organisations invest heavily in one while neglecting the other: process workshops without management support, or management structures (labs, budgets, titles) without process skills. Ask participants where they think their own organisation currently sits.
