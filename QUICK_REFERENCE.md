# Innovation Literacy - Facilitator Quick Reference

One-page reference for live workshop delivery. Keep this visible during sessions.

---

## The 11 Elements (One-Line Each)

1. **Process** - Structured, gate-based activity (Design Thinking, Double Diamond)
2. **Interventions** - Stage-specific instructions that prevent common failure modes
3. **Tools** - Physical and digital enablers (matched to stage fidelity)
4. **Skills** - Learnable capabilities (analytical, creative, communication, systems thinking, experimentation)
5. **Mindset** - Cognitive orientations (curiosity, growth, tolerance for uncertainty, challenging assumptions)
6. **Behaviours** - Observable actions (active listening, constructive challenge, sharing work-in-progress)
7. **Environment** - Container protecting innovation from operational gravity
8. **Collaboration** - Team dynamics (psychological safety, cognitive diversity, constructive conflict)
9. **Facilitation** - Meta-capability orchestrating all other elements
10. **Governance** - Bidirectional communication legitimising innovation organisationally
11. **Resources** - Time, budget, people, materials (continuously defended)

---

## Key Interventions by Phase

| Phase | Purpose | Interventions | Common Failure Mode |
|-------|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Empathise** | Understand users | "Dig deeper", "Ask why 5x", "Show me", silence discipline | Surface inquiry, assumption validation |
| **Define** | Frame problem | How Might We, assumption mapping, problem space mapping | Solution-jumping, weak framing |
| **Ideate** | Generate concepts | "Don't explain/defend/evaluate", quantity targets, "build on that" | Premature evaluation, first idea wins |
| **Prototype** | Make tangible | "Build it in 7 min", "it should break", "riskiest assumption" | Over-polishing, perfectionism |
| **Test** | Generate evidence | "Don't defend prototype", "what did you notice?", think-aloud | Confirmation bias, defensive positioning |

## Divergent vs Convergent (Critical Distinction)

**Divergent (Opening):**
- **Purpose:** Explore widely, generate options, defer judgement
- **When:** Discover, early Define, Ideate, early Prototype
- **Rules:** No evaluation, build on ideas, quantity over quality, "yes, and..."
- **Failure:** Evaluating too soon kills exploration

**Convergent (Closing):**
- **Purpose:** Narrow, decide, commit, focus
- **When:** End of Define, post-Ideate, Evaluation, stage gates
- **Rules:** Apply criteria, assess evidence, make decisions
- **Failure:** Converging too soon produces narrow solutions

**Rhythm:** Open → Close → Open → Close (throughout process)

## Common Failure Modes & Fixes

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | Quick Fix |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Solution-jumping** | Proposing features during discovery | "We're still in problem space - park that solution for later" |
| **Premature convergence** | Evaluating first idea in ideation | "We need 20 more ideas before we evaluate" |
| **Cargo-culting** | Process rituals without understanding | "What are we learning? What assumption are we testing?" |
| **HiPPO dominance** | Highest-paid person's opinion wins | Silent individual work before group discussion, structured turn-taking |
| **Operational gravity** | Innovation time reclaimed | Escalate to governance, cite charter |
| **Defensive testing** | Explaining/rescuing prototype | "Don't speak - just observe and document" |

## If You Only Remember 3 Things Per Element

**1. Process:** Gated not linear, failure = loop back, creates learning not deliverables  
**2. Interventions:** Apply at exact moment of behaviour formation, enforce consistently  
**3. Tools:** Match fidelity to stage (low early, high late)  

**4. Skills:** All learnable through practice, not innate  
**5. Mindset:** Shaped by environment, not personality  
**6. Behaviours:** Active listening foundation for all others  

**7. Environment:** Must protect from operational culture or innovation dies  
**8. Collaboration:** Psychological safety is prerequisite for everything else  
**9. Facilitation:** Hold process, stay content-neutral, interrupt violations immediately  

**10. Governance:** Without it, innovation crushed by operations inevitably  
**11. Resources:** Continuously contested, validated through evidence

## Facilitator Mantras

- **"Name the phase explicitly"** - Teams must know where they are
- **"Intervene immediately when patterns violated"** - Don't let bad habits form
- **"Hold the groan zone"** - Discomfort means you're doing it right
- **"Protect divergence from evaluation"** - They're incompatible modes
- **"Make thinking visible"** - Ideas in heads can't be built upon
- **"Celebrate invalidation"** - Failed tests = successful learning

---

## Warning Signs to Watch For

- Innovation time "borrowed" for operational work
- Demands for ROI during discovery phase
- Failure punished rather than learned from
- Middle management blocking not enabling
- Governance meetings = status reports not decisions
- "Innovation" becoming theatre not substance

**Action:** Surface pattern to group, escalate if systemic

## Stage-Gate Questions

Before progressing, ask:

**Discovery → Define:**
- Do we genuinely understand user needs?
- Have we moved beyond surface symptoms?
- What did we learn that surprised us?

**Define → Ideate:**
- Is problem comprehensively framed?
- Have stakeholders aligned on problem statement?
- What assumptions underpin our framing?

**Ideate → Prototype:**
- Have we generated sufficient range?
- Which concepts survived initial screening?
- What's the riskiest assumption to test?

**Prototype → Test:**
- Is prototype appropriate fidelity for what we're testing?
- What specific question does this test answer?
- How will we recognise success vs failure?

**Test → Decision:**
- What did we learn (not: what did we build)?
- Which assumptions validated? Invalidated?
- What evidence supports next investment?

---

## Emergency Interventions

**If energy crashes:** Break + movement, don't power through  
**If dominance emerges:** Structured turn-taking, breakout rooms  
**If stuck in divergence:** "Time to converge - we have enough material"  
**If premature convergence:** "Open back up - we need more options"  
**If defending prototypes:** "Remember: it should break, that's success"  
**If losing process:** "Where are we in the Double Diamond right now?"
